Data Journalism: 2014 State Economic Factors

Understanding multi-axis data fields

X - Axis

  • Educaton < Highschool (%): Default x-axis option. % of population with less than a high school education.
  • Education > Bachelors (%): Second x-axis option. % of population with less than a Bachelors Degree.
  • No Health Coverage (%): Third x-axis option. % of population with no healthcare coverage.

Y - Axis

  • Cannot afford Healthcare (%): Default y-axis option. % of population which cannot afford healthcare.
  • Unemployment (%): Second y-axis option. % of population older than 16 and unemployed.
  • Depression(%): Third y-axis option. % of population with known depression.

Findings

  • There appears to be a strong correlation within the population of persons with less than a high school education not being able to afford healthcare.
  • There appears to be a strong correlation between a lower state unemployment level and percent of population with at least a graduate degree.
  • Further study would be required, but there appears to be a moderate correlation between persons with no healthcare coverage and depression levels.
